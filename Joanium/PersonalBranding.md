A personal brand is not a performance — it's the clearest, most strategic expression of who you actually are and what you uniquely offer. The goal is not to become famous. It's to be findable, credible, and compelling to the exact right people.

## Phase 1: Brand Foundation

Before writing a single word of content, establish the core:

### Positioning Statement (internal compass, not public copy)
```
I help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome] through [your unique approach/method].
Unlike [alternatives they might consider], I [key differentiator].

Example:
"I help early-stage SaaS founders build their first sales motion through 
founder-led selling. Unlike traditional sales consultants, I've actually 
done this 3 times myself with no sales team."
```

### Core Brand Pillars (2-4 topics you will consistently own)
```
Ask: What are the 3 topics at the intersection of:
- What you know deeply (expertise)
- What you're genuinely passionate about (sustainability)
- What your target audience cares about (relevance)

Example pillars:
- Technical leadership: how engineering managers become executives
- Team building: hiring, culture, and retaining great engineers  
- Career transitions: pivoting between IC and management roles

Everything you create should connect to at least one pillar.
```

### Voice & Tone Definition
```
Choose 5 adjectives that describe how you communicate:
[Direct / Warm / Irreverent / Rigorous / Accessible / Bold / Thoughtful / ...]

What is NOT your voice? (equally important — defines the boundaries)
[Not: corporate-speak / not: self-promotional / not: preachy]

Find your vocal signature — the one thing people would recognize as distinctly you:
Examples: "I always use specific numbers" / "I always include what failed, not just what worked"
         "I always take the contrarian view first" / "I always connect theory to a specific story"
```

## Phase 2: LinkedIn Profile Optimization

LinkedIn is the highest-ROI platform for most professional brands. Optimize every field:

### Headline (220 characters — your most important line)
```
❌ Weak:  "Marketing Manager at Acme Corp"
❌ Weak:  "Passionate about driving growth and innovation"
✓ Strong: "Helping B2B SaaS companies build content engines that generate pipeline | 3x founder | Writing about growth tactics that actually work"

Formula options:
[What you do] + [For whom] + [Key credential or differentiator]
[Result you create] + [How] + [Social proof]
[Role] + [Specific focus] + [Platform promise] ("Writing about X weekly")
```

### About Section (2,600 characters — tell your story)
```
Structure:
1. Opening hook (1-2 sentences that stop the scroll — counterintuitive, specific, or compelling)
2. Who you are + what you do (the positioning in natural language)
3. Why it matters / your origin story (what drove you here)
4. What you've built/done (specific results, not job descriptions)
5. What you're working on now
6. Clear call to action (what should they do next?)

Length: 250-400 words. Paragraphs of 2-3 sentences. Scannable.
Write in first person. Sound human. Not corporate.
```

### Experience Section
```
Each role: 
- Title | Company | Dates
- One line: what the company does (don't assume they know)
- 3-5 bullets: specific results with numbers, not responsibilities
  ❌ "Responsible for managing the marketing team"
  ✓  "Built the marketing team from 2 to 12 people; pipeline grew 340% YoY"
- Include: projects, launches, initiatives with quantifiable outcomes
```

### Featured Section
Use for: best LinkedIn posts, articles, speaking videos, portfolios, case studies, notable press.
This is prime real estate — curate ruthlessly.

## Phase 3: Content Strategy

### The Content Matrix
```
4 post types to rotate through:

1. TEACH — Share knowledge (40% of content)
   "Here's how I approached [problem]"
   "3 things I learned from [experience]"
   "The framework I use for [decision]"

2. STORY — Share experience (30% of content)
   "The time I failed at [X] and what it taught me"
   "How I got from [A] to [B]"
   "What nobody tells you about [experience]"

3. OPINION — Take a stand (20% of content)
   "Hot take: [contrarian view] — here's why"
   "[Common advice] is wrong. Here's what actually works."
   "I disagree with [conventional wisdom]"

4. ENGAGE — Start conversation (10% of content)
   Questions to your audience
   Polls on relevant topics
   Responding to trends in your industry
```

### Post Anatomy (LinkedIn)
```
LINE 1: The hook (most important line — determines if they click "see more")
         - Lead with the counterintuitive
         - Lead with a specific number
         - Lead with a provocative question
         - Lead with a mini-story opening

LINES 2-5: Expand the hook — establish the problem or setup

BODY: Deliver the value
      - Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)
      - White space between every paragraph
      - Use numbered lists or line breaks for scanability

ENDING: 
      - Concrete takeaway or summary
      - Optional: question to invite comment engagement

LENGTH: 
      - Short (under 150 words): pure insight or opinion
      - Medium (150-300 words): teaching post with structure
      - Long (300-500 words): storytelling post
      - Avoid > 500 words unless newsletter-style
```

### Content Calendar Framework
```
Weekly rhythm:
- 3-5 posts per week is the optimal range for LinkedIn growth
- Monday: Industry insight or opinion (start the week with substance)
- Wednesday: Story or lesson from your own experience
- Friday: Tactical framework or how-to

Batch creation:
- Write 2 weeks of posts in one 2-hour session
- Use the "Content Vault" — save every good idea as a draft
- Repurpose one long piece into 3-5 shorter posts
```

## Phase 4: Multi-Platform Presence

**Platform selection — focus beats spreading thin:**
```
LinkedIn:   B2B, professional reputation, hiring/being hired, thought leadership
Twitter/X:  Tech, media, investors, real-time conversation, raw thinking
Newsletter: Deep relationship with audience you own (not platform-dependent)
YouTube:    Long-form authority, high production bar, search discovery
Podcast:    Audio audience, long-form conversations, guest networking
Instagram:  Visual brands, consumer products, lifestyle businesses
```

**Bio consistency across platforms:**
```
One-liner (for Twitter bio, email signature, speaker intro):
[What you do] + [Who for] + [Proof point or link]
"Founder @Acme | Writing about founder-led sales | 2x exit"

Medium bio (for About pages, podcast introductions):
Paragraph 1: What you do and who you serve
Paragraph 2: Your credibility and background
Paragraph 3: What you're building/working on now + CTA

Full bio (for press, speaking, formal contexts):
Third-person. 250-400 words. Lead with current role.
Include: key credentials, notable outcomes, personal dimension, CTA.
```

## Phase 5: Brand Audit Checklist

Review every 6 months:
- [ ] LinkedIn headline reflects current positioning and value
- [ ] About section sounds like you, not a job description
- [ ] Profile photo is current and professional
- [ ] Featured section has your best recent work
- [ ] Content pillars are still relevant and authentic
- [ ] Voice is consistent across platforms
- [ ] You can state your positioning in one sentence
- [ ] Top 10 posts — do they represent how you want to be known?
