Platform Product Post-Launch Analysis is an expert product-management skill for Platform Product. The focal concern is Post-Launch Analysis, but the real bar is decision quality that survives ambiguity, disagreement, and post-launch accountability.

## When To Use

- Use this when Platform Product Post-Launch Analysis affects what the team works on, how it decides, or how it learns from outcomes.
- Use this when activity is high but the decision model is weak or inconsistent.
- Use this when disagreement is expensive because stakes, owners, or metrics are fuzzy.
- Use this when post-launch learning needs to be designed before the launch itself.

## Core Principles

- Good product work starts by naming the decision, not by defending a preferred solution.
- Metrics are only useful if they explain tradeoffs, not just movement.
- Alignment comes from explicit assumptions and ownership, not from more meetings.
- Rollouts and post-launch analysis are part of product design, not cleanup.
- Research, support, and operational evidence should challenge roadmap confidence constantly.

- What decision inside Platform Product Post-Launch Analysis is actually being made, and what would make it reversible?

## Workflow

1. Frame the problem, the desired outcome, and the decision horizon in plain language.
2. Map stakeholders, assumptions, constraints, and the evidence needed to decide cleanly.
3. Choose the metric model, rollout shape, and feedback loops before execution begins.
4. Make ownership and decision rights explicit so disagreement can converge.
5. Run the rollout with instrumentation and checkpoints that surface surprises early.
6. Complete the loop with post-launch analysis that changes future product behavior.

## Artifacts

- A decision memo for Platform Product Post-Launch Analysis with clear tradeoffs.
- A metric and rollout note tied to the chosen outcome.
- A stakeholder map or risk review for the contentious parts of the decision.
- A post-launch analysis prompt set that prevents shallow retrospectives.

## Tradeoffs

- Speed of decision versus quality of evidence.
- Broad alignment versus clarity of ownership.
- Experiment breadth versus learning depth.
- Short-term wins versus the operating cost they introduce later.

## Signals To Watch

- Decision latency and rework caused by missing assumptions.
- Metric movement tied to actual behavior change rather than noise.
- Stakeholder escalation patterns that reveal weak alignment.
- Launch surprises that should have been discovered earlier.
- Whether post-launch learning changes future prioritization.

## Review Checklist

- [ ] The decision inside Platform Product Post-Launch Analysis is explicit.
- [ ] Metrics and tradeoffs are defined before rollout.
- [ ] Ownership and stakeholder roles are clear.
- [ ] Feedback and post-launch analysis are part of the plan.
- [ ] The team can explain why this work matters now.
- [ ] The operating cadence is strong enough to revisit bad assumptions quickly.

## Common Failure Modes

- Mistaking discussion volume for product rigor.
- Launching without a serious post-launch learning loop.
- Choosing metrics that flatter the plan rather than challenge it.
- Calling a stakeholder problem a roadmap problem.
