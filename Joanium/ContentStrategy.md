Content strategy is the systematic plan for creating, distributing, and managing content that serves both your audience's needs and your business goals. Great content strategy is audience-first, goal-driven, and ruthlessly prioritized.

## Phase 1: Foundation

### Audience Definition (most important step)
```
Primary audience profile:
- Demographics: [Role, age range, industry, company size, geography]
- Goals: What are they trying to achieve professionally/personally?
- Challenges: What obstacles do they face?
- Information diet: What do they currently read/watch/listen to?
- Content behavior: How do they discover, consume, and share content?
- Vocabulary: What terms do THEY use? (Use their words, not yours)

Pain point hierarchy:
1. Urgent & important (on fire right now)
2. Important but not urgent (persistent background problem)
3. Aspirational (want to achieve, not actively struggling)

Create content for all three — but lead with #1.
```

### Content Mission Statement
```
Formula:
"[Brand] creates [content type] for [specific audience] to help them [achieve outcome]."

Example:
"Stripe creates technical guides and business essays for founders and developers 
to help them build and scale internet businesses."

Your content mission answers:
- Who is this for?
- What kind of content do we make?
- What value does it create for them?
- What business purpose does it serve us?
```

### Business Goals Mapping
```
Map each content type to a specific business goal:

Awareness content     → Reach new audiences, build brand recognition
                        Metrics: Impressions, reach, new visitors, social shares

Consideration content → Nurture interest, differentiate, build trust
                        Metrics: Engagement, return visits, email signups, time on page

Conversion content    → Drive specific action
                        Metrics: Leads, trials, purchases, demos booked

Retention content     → Deepen customer relationship, reduce churn
                        Metrics: Open rates, NPS, feature adoption, renewal rate

SEO content           → Organic discovery
                        Metrics: Rankings, organic traffic, backlinks
```

## Phase 2: Content Pillars

Content pillars are the 3-5 core topic areas you own. Everything you create lives under a pillar.

```
Pillar selection criteria:
1. Audience cares deeply about it
2. You have genuine expertise or perspective
3. It connects to your product/service (at least tangentially)
4. It's differentiated — you have something to say others aren't saying

Pillar structure:
Each pillar should have:
- A clear topic boundary (what it includes and excludes)
- A point of view (your unique angle or stance on this topic)
- A format preference (long-form guide? Case study? Data report?)
- 10-20 potential content ideas already identified

Example pillars for a project management tool:
1. Team productivity: Remote work, async communication, focus
2. Engineering leadership: Scaling teams, technical decisions, 1:1s
3. Product process: Planning, prioritization, roadmapping
4. Company building: Hiring, culture, organizational design
```

## Phase 3: Content Types & Formats

### The Content Pyramid
```
CORNERSTONE CONTENT (few, high-effort, long-lasting)
→ Long-form guides, research reports, comprehensive resources
→ Target: ranks for competitive keywords, builds authority, attracts backlinks
→ Cadence: 1-2x per month

REGULAR CONTENT (consistent cadence, medium effort)
→ Blog posts, newsletters, video series, podcast episodes
→ Target: builds audience habit, demonstrates expertise, drives email growth
→ Cadence: 1-3x per week

REACTIVE CONTENT (fast, timely, low-effort)
→ Social posts, news commentary, quick tips, responses to trends
→ Target: algorithm favor, real-time relevance, engagement
→ Cadence: daily
```

### Format Selection Guide
```
Long-form guides (2,000-5,000 words):
Best for: high-intent SEO, comprehensive topic coverage, thought leadership
Structure: Problem → Why it's hard → Framework → Detailed how-to → Examples → Summary

Case studies:
Best for: conversion, credibility, showing real results
Structure: Challenge → Context → Solution → Results → Lessons

Data reports / original research:
Best for: backlink generation, PR, authority positioning
Structure: Key findings first → Methodology → Deep dive by section → Implications

Email newsletters:
Best for: owned audience, relationship depth, consistent touchpoints
Format options: Curated links with commentary / Original essay / Roundup / How-to

Video (YouTube/LinkedIn):
Best for: high-engagement topics, tutorials, personality-driven brands
Sweet spots: Educational (how-to), Entertaining (POV stories), Inspiring (transformation)

Podcast:
Best for: deep relationship with audience, guest access, long-form exploration
Format options: Interview / Solo essay / Panel / Narrative
```

## Phase 4: SEO Integration

### Keyword Strategy
```
Keyword research process:
1. Start with your audience's vocabulary (not yours)
2. Map keywords to buyer journey stage:
   Awareness: "what is [problem]", "why does [problem] happen"
   Consideration: "how to [solve problem]", "[tool type] for [use case]"
   Decision: "[product] alternative", "[product] review", "best [tool type]"

3. Prioritize by: search volume × relevance × winnable (your domain authority vs. competition)
4. Group by intent: informational, commercial, navigational

Content-keyword mapping:
- One primary keyword per piece (clear focus)
- 3-5 secondary/semantic keywords (natural language variation)
- Never keyword-stuff — write for the human, optimize for the algorithm
```

### On-Page SEO Checklist
```
- [ ] Primary keyword in: title tag, H1, first 100 words, URL slug
- [ ] Title tag 50-60 characters, includes keyword + compelling reason to click
- [ ] Meta description 150-160 characters, summarizes and compels
- [ ] H2/H3 headings are logical, include semantic keywords
- [ ] Internal links to 3-5 related pieces
- [ ] Images: alt text describes image + keyword where natural
- [ ] Content covers topic comprehensively (matches search intent)
- [ ] Mobile-readable formatting (short paragraphs, bullets, headers)
- [ ] Page load speed is acceptable
```

## Phase 5: Distribution Strategy

### The 3x Distribution Rule
Every piece of content should be distributed in at least 3 ways:

```
Primary publication → Secondary formats → Amplification

Example:
Long-form guide (primary)
→ Email newsletter featuring key insights (secondary)
→ LinkedIn post with key takeaway + link (secondary)
→ 3 Twitter/X posts with individual insights over 3 weeks (secondary)
→ Outreach to 5 relevant sites for potential republication or backlinks (amplification)
```

### Distribution Channel Matrix
```
Channel         | Best for                    | Format        | Frequency
----------------|------------------------------|---------------|----------
Email list      | Owned audience, depth        | Newsletter    | 1-2x/week
LinkedIn        | B2B, professional topics     | Post/article  | Daily
Twitter/X       | Tech, media, real-time       | Thread/tweet  | Daily
YouTube         | Tutorial, high engagement    | Video         | 1-2x/week
Podcast         | Deep relationship            | Episode       | Weekly
SEO             | Long-tail, evergreen         | Article       | 2-4x/month
Community       | Niche, high-trust            | Discussion    | As relevant
```

## Phase 6: Editorial Calendar

### 3-Month Rolling Calendar Structure
```
Month 1: Foundation
- Week 1: Flagship cornerstone content (high SEO value)
- Week 2: Newsletter + 3 social posts
- Week 3: Supporting content piece (complements cornerstone)
- Week 4: Case study or customer story

Month 2: Amplification
- Repurpose Month 1 content across channels
- Publish second pillar cornerstone
- Begin link building for Month 1 pieces

Month 3: Evaluation + Iteration
- Review: What performed? What didn't?
- Double down on top-performing topics/formats
- Prune or update underperforming content
```

## Phase 7: Measurement Framework

```
North Star metric: [The one metric that best indicates content program success]
Examples: Organic traffic growth / Email subscriber growth / Inbound pipeline influenced

Supporting metrics by goal:
Awareness:     Organic impressions, social reach, backlinks earned
Engagement:    Time on page, pages per session, email open rates, social engagement
Conversion:    Email signups, demo requests, content-influenced pipeline
Retention:     Newsletter retention rate, repeat visitors, NPS correlation

Review cadence:
- Weekly: Publish, distribute, monitor engagement
- Monthly: Analyze performance, adjust topics/formats
- Quarterly: Audit full strategy, reassess pillars, update keyword targets
- Annually: Full content audit, strategic reset
```
